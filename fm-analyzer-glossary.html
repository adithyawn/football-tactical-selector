‚ö° 1. Attacking Creativity Metrics

Pass Completion Ratio (%)

Definition: Percentage of successful passes compared to total passes attempted by the team.

Why Important: Indicates ball retention and passing accuracy. Higher pass completion enables possession-based play and reduces turnovers.

Range Rating Description
< 75% Poor Struggling with basic passing execution
75-82% Average Standard passing accuracy
82-88% Good Strong passing consistency
> 88% Elite Exceptional ball retention (Man City, Barcelona level)

Key Passes per 90 Minutes

Definition: Number of passes that directly lead to a shot attempt.

Why Important: Primary indicator of chance creation quality. Teams with high key passes create more goal-scoring opportunities.

Range Rating Description
< 6 Very Low Lacking creativity - teams score 35% fewer goals (Oberstone, 2009)
6-10 Average Competitive level chance creation
10-12 Good Solid chance creation capability
> 12 Elite Top 15% of teams (Man City 2018: 18.3, De Bruyne: 3.8 individual)

Research: Rein & Memmert (2016) - Key passes correlate with +0.18 goals/game per additional key pass

Open Play xA (Expected Assists) per 90

Definition: Expected goals value of chances created from open play (excluding set pieces).

Why Important: Measures quality of chances created, not just quantity. A team can have many key passes but low xA if chances are low quality.

Range Rating Description
< 0.8 Poor Low-quality chance creation
0.8-1.2 Average Standard chance quality
1.2-1.6 Good Creating dangerous opportunities consistently
> 1.6 Elite World-class chance creation (Man City 2017-19: 1.9)

Research: Caley (2015), StatsBomb - xA is strongest predictor of future attacking success

Progressive Passes per 90

Definition: Passes that move the ball significantly closer to opponent's goal (at least 10 yards toward goal).

Why Important: Indicates ability to break lines and advance play vertically rather than sideways passing.

Range Rating Description
< 6 Poor Sideways passing, no penetration
6-9 Average Decent vertical progression
9-12 Good Strong line-breaking ability
> 12 Elite Exceptional vertical play (√òdegaard: 10.2, Bernardo Silva: 11.8)

Research: Power et al. (2017), Sarmento et al. (2018) - Progressive passing increases goal probability by 23%

Dribbles per 90 Minutes

Definition: Number of successful dribbles past an opponent per 90 minutes.

Why Important: Indicates individual creativity and ability to unbalance defenses when passing lanes are closed.

Range Rating Description
< 4 Poor Limited 1v1 capability
4-7 Average Standard dribbling threat
7-10 Good Strong individual creativity
> 10 Elite World-class dribblers (Vinicius Jr: 12.3, Saka: 10.8)

Research: Sarmento et al. (2018), Tenga et al. (2010) - Successful dribbles in final third increase goal probability by 31%

Conversion Rate (%)

Definition: Percentage of shots that result in goals (Goals / Total Shots √ó 100).

Why Important: Measures finishing efficiency. Teams can create many chances but still lose if conversion is poor.

Range Rating Description
< 8% Poor Wasteful finishing, poor shot selection
8-11% Average League average conversion
11-14% Good Clinical finishing
> 14% Elite World-class finishing (Haaland: 22%, Bayern 2020: 16%)

Research: Anderson & Sally (2013) - Conversion rate is most volatile metric, can fluctuate 4-6% season to season

üõ°Ô∏è 2. Defensive Creativity Metrics

Interceptions per 90 Minutes

Definition: Number of times defenders intercept opponent passes per 90 minutes.

Why Important: Indicates defensive awareness and proactive defending. High interceptions prevent attacks before they develop.

Range Rating Description Analysis Shown
< 2.5 Poor Passive defending - reactive rather than proactive WEAKNESS: Low Defensive Interception Rate
2.5-4.0 Average Decent anticipation, room for improvement AVERAGE: Defensive Interception - Room for Improvement
> 4.0 Elite Exceptional defensive awareness (Kant√© 2016: 6.4) STRENGTH: Elite Defensive Anticipation

Research: Mackenzie & Cushion (2013), UEFA Technical Reports - Teams with <2.5 interceptions/game face 35% more dangerous attacks

Defender Pass Completion (%)

Definition: Pass completion percentage specifically for center-backs and full-backs.

Why Important: Modern football requires defenders to initiate attacks. Low pass completion from defenders forces risky long balls.

Range Rating Description
< 80% Poor Uncomfortable on ball, forces long clearances
80-88% Average Decent ball-playing ability
88-92% Good Confident ball-playing defenders
> 92% Elite Elite ball progression from back (Van Dijk: 92.8%, Stones: 93.4%)

Research: Fernandez-Navarro et al. (2018) - High defender pass completion correlates with +8.3% possession

Defender Progressive Passes per 90

Definition: Number of progressive passes (10+ yards toward goal) made by defenders per 90 minutes.

Why Important: Key metric for build-up play quality. Defenders who can progress the ball bypass opponent press effectively.

Range Rating Description Analysis Shown
< 4.5 Poor Limited buildup capability, 28% more long balls PRIORITY 2B: Defensive Creativity Deficit
4.5-6.5 Average Decent ball progression from back (No specific recommendation)
> 6.5 Elite Top 15% buildup capability (Van Dijk: 7.2, Stones: 7.9) STRENGTH: Elite Defensive Creativity

Research: Power et al. (2017), Fernandez-Navarro et al. (2018) - Defender progressive passes correlate with +0.18 goals/game

Defender Dribbles per 90

Definition: Successful dribbles by defenders (center-backs and full-backs) per 90 minutes.

Why Important: Shows defenders' ability to carry the ball out of pressure and advance play without passing.

Range Rating Description
< 1.0 Average Standard central defender (rarely dribbles)
1.0-2.0 Good Comfortable carrying ball forward
> 2.0 Elite Modern ball-carrying defenders (Laporte: 2.3, TAA: 3.1)

Defender Assists per 90

Definition: Goal assists provided by defenders per 90 minutes.

Why Important: Shows attacking contribution from defense, especially from full-backs or wing-backs.

Range Rating Description
< 0.1 Average Standard defensive role
0.1-0.2 Good Attacking full-backs contributing to attack
> 0.2 Elite Elite attacking defenders (TAA: 0.28, Robertson: 0.22)

Fullback Cross Completion (%)

Definition: Percentage of successful crosses delivered by full-backs and wing-backs.

Why Important: Critical for wide-based attacks. Low cross completion wastes possession and attacking opportunities.

Range Rating Description
< 20% Poor Wasteful wide play
20-28% Average Standard crossing efficiency
28-35% Good Quality crossing delivery
> 35% Elite Exceptional crossing (TAA: 38%, Cancelo: 36%)

Research: Tenga et al. (2010) - Cross completion >30% increases goal probability by 18%

üìä 3. General Performance Metrics

Expected Goals (xG) per Game

Definition: Statistical measure of shot quality - probability that a shot will result in a goal based on historical data.

Why Important: Best predictor of future performance. Teams consistently outperforming xG are "lucky" and will regress to mean.

Range Rating Description
< 1.0 Poor Low-quality chances, defensive struggles expected
1.0-1.5 Average Mid-table attacking performance
1.5-2.0 Good Top-half attacking quality
> 2.0 Elite Elite attacking team (Man City 2018: 2.47, Liverpool 2019: 2.38)

Research: Caley (2015), StatsBomb - xG has 0.75 correlation with final league position

Expected Goals Against (xGA) per Game

Definition: Quality of chances conceded to opponents measured by xG.

Why Important: Best indicator of defensive quality independent of goalkeeper performance.

Range Rating Description
< 0.9 Elite World-class defense (Liverpool 2019: 0.88, Atletico 2021: 0.82)
0.9-1.2 Good Strong defensive structure
1.2-1.5 Average Mid-table defensive quality
> 1.5 Poor Porous defense, high relegation risk

Shots on Target %

Definition: Percentage of total shots that are on target (would go in if not saved).

Why Important: Indicates shot selection quality. Higher percentage means better shooting positions and decision-making.

Range Rating Description
< 30% Poor Poor shot selection, shooting from low-probability areas
30-36% Average Standard shot accuracy
36-42% Good Good shot selection and positioning
> 42% Elite Exceptional shot quality (Barcelona 2011: 43.8%)

Research: Hughes & Franks (2005), Sarmento et al. (2014)

Clean Sheets %

Definition: Percentage of matches where team concedes zero goals.

Why Important: Strong indicator of defensive organization and consistency. Winning teams average 40%+ clean sheets.

Range Rating Description
< 25% Poor Defensive fragility
25-35% Average Mid-table defensive record
35-45% Good Solid defensive organization
> 45% Elite Exceptional defensive record (Chelsea 2005: 55%)

‚ö° 4. Pressing & Defensive Metrics

PPDA (Passes Allowed Per Defensive Action)

Definition: Number of opponent passes allowed before making a defensive action (tackle, interception, foul). Lower = more aggressive pressing.

Why Important: Key metric for pressing intensity. Shows how high and aggressively team defends.

Range Rating Description
< 8 Ultra-aggressive High press - Liverpool 2018-20: 7.2, Leeds 2020-21: 8.9
8-15 Balanced Mid-block pressing - sustainable and effective
15-20 Low block Defensive setup, counter-attacking style
> 20 Passive Very deep defending, face 4.2+ more shots/game

Research: Fernandez-Navarro et al. (2016, 2018) - Teams with PPDA >18 concede 0.31 more goals/game

Defensive Line Height

Definition: Average position of defensive line measured in yards from own goal.

Why Important: Determines defensive strategy - high line enables pressing but risks being caught, low line is safer but allows opponent possession.

Range Rating Description
> 50 yards Very High Aggressive pressing system (requires pace and discipline)
45-50 yards High Modern attacking team setup
40-45 yards Medium Balanced defensive approach
< 40 yards Deep Defensive/counter-attacking setup

High Turnovers (Possession Wins in Attacking Third)

Definition: Number of times team wins possession in opponent's attacking third.

Why Important: Winning ball high up pitch creates immediate goal-scoring opportunities. High turnovers correlate with +0.45 goals/game.

Range Rating Description
< 3 Poor Passive defensive approach
3-5 Average Standard pressing frequency
5-7 Good Active high press
> 7 Elite Ultra-aggressive pressing (Liverpool 2019: 8.3)

Research: Rein & Memmert (2016) - High turnovers increase goal probability by 42%

üó∫Ô∏è 5. Passing Patterns & Heat Map

Passing Zone Distribution

Definition: Percentage breakdown of where team completes passes across the pitch.

Zones:

  • Defensive Left/Right: Own third, wide areas
  • Midfield Left/Center/Right: Middle third distribution
  • Attacking Left/Center/Right: Final third penetration

Critical Benchmarks:

Zone Elite Range Interpretation
Defensive Zones 15-25% Efficient buildup without excessive back-passing
Midfield Center 20-30% Central control - most important zone for ball progression
Attacking Third Total > 22% Good final third penetration
Attacking Third < 15% CRITICAL: Stuck in midfield, can't penetrate

Research: Sarmento et al. (2014) - Teams with <6% attacking third passes average only 0.9 goals/game

Width vs Central Balance

Definition: Ratio of passes through wide areas vs central channels.

Pattern Characteristics Example Teams
Wide-Heavy (60%+) Reliance on wings, crossing-based attack Liverpool 2019-21, Real Madrid
Balanced (40-60%) Flexible, can attack through any channel Man City, Bayern Munich
Central-Heavy (60%+) Possession-based, central overloads Barcelona (Guardiola era)
